  • Food for Thought

    I'm addicted to food shows.

    No Reservations with Anthony Bourdain, Kitchen Nightmares (the British version) with Gordon Ramsay, and Bizzare Foods with Andrew Zimmern are incredibly entertaining programs that at the same time make my mouth water like Niagra Falls.

    Honestly, how can people watch these shows and NOT be extremely jealous of these individuals?! They've got it made!

    I've learned a lot from these shows about the kitchen. Cooking is something I've always wanted to learn about and I always make an effort to learn new things.

    So learn with me!

    Recipe: Steak

    A steak should not be daunting to cook. In fact, it is extremely easy. Almost too easy for how delicious it is.

    1. Prep

    You'll need: a steak (preferably at room temperature), butter, oil, salt and pepper, and a pan. That's it!

    2. Heat

    Heat up the pan. Put in the oil. Get the oil smoking.

    3. Season

    While heating the pan, rub the steak with salt and pepper.

    4. Cook

    When the oil starts to smoke, throw in the steak. Make sure to lay down the steak evenly in the middle of the pan. Cook each side of the steak 2-3 minutes. ONLY TURN THE STEAK ONCE!

    5. Butter

    When the steak is about a minute away from finishing, throw in the butter. This will give the steak a nice nut-brown finish. Baste the steak to finish it off.

    6. Rest

    Use a little of that leftover butter and pour it on top of the steak. Take it out of the pan. Throw it on a plate to rest.

    7. Enjoy

    Cut the steak in half-inch slices. Enjoy!

    That wasn't too bad, right?

    After learning how to cook a steak, I became a much more confident "chef," if you will. It's the little victories in everything I try to learn that make it worth while.

    The best part about learning how to cook is the delicious by-products. Obviously.
